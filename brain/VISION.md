# Res Publica — Vision

*The condensed north star. Full 15-section elaboration lives at `PRODUCTS/product-vision.md` — this file exists so a reader gets the shape of the vision in two minutes rather than needing the whole document.*

## The north star

Res Publica becomes the civic organization where showing up informed is no longer a privilege of people with time to read annual reports — where a citizen in Munich, a researcher in Berlin, and a Persian-speaking diaspora member abroad can each ask their own question of the same trustworthy institutional record, in their own language, and get an answer that moves them one step closer to participating rather than one step closer to disengaging.

## What actually changes

Today, Res Publica *publishes to* a public — one document, one dialogue, one dataset, serving everyone equally and therefore no one precisely. An AI-first Res Publica *meets* each citizen, researcher, and policymaker where their actual question is, while publishing everything as openly as it does today and measuring itself exactly as it does today (see `MISSION.md`). Concretely:

1. **Participation becomes two-way.** A citizen can ask what previous rounds of a citizens' dialogue concluded, in their own language, before ever attending one — grounded in the org's own published record.
2. **Open data stops requiring a one-off pilot to become legible.** Understandability becomes a standing platform capability, not a single completed project.
3. **Research and policy become navigable by relevance, not publication date.** A finding and the policy position it supports find the person who needs them, instead of waiting to be found.

## The one positioning discipline that governs all of it

**AI is the plumbing, not the pitch.** Res Publica's audience is disposed to distrust hype — leading with "AI-first" imports exactly the credibility problem this organization exists to be an alternative to. Every public mention of AI names the civic job it does, never the technology for its own sake. See `CONSTITUTION.md` §1.
