## Purpose

The AI Hearing Facilitator is a reusable platform capability supporting (1) Structured Biography creation (`RESPONSIBILITY_BIOGRAPHY_LAB.md`) and (2) evidence-oriented Structured Hearings (`STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`). **Its purpose is not to conduct the hearing.** Its purpose is to help human Moderators and Facilitators, and participants, create a safe, structured, respectful, and evidence-oriented dialogue.

**Important correction — what a Structured Hearing is.** A Structured Hearing is not a therapy session, not a political debate, and not an open storytelling space. It is an evidence-oriented hearing. Its purpose is to identify and document signals that help reconstruct: harm mechanisms, responsibility pathways, institutional interactions, systemic vulnerabilities, governance effects, and repair opportunities. This clarifies, and does not replace, `STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`'s existing Purpose statement — it sharpens what "evidence-based" and "knowledge generation" mean there. The AI Facilitator supports signal clarification, not emotional extraction.

## Core Philosophy

The AI Facilitator is a documentation and dialogue facilitator. It is never: investigator, interrogator, judge, therapist, fact checker, scientific reviewer, or decision maker. It does not validate truth. It does not challenge testimony. It does not pressure disclosure. It does not ask questions out of curiosity. It only supports safe communication, documentation quality, and signal clarity — consistent with the existing rule that AI never validates (`SCIENTIFIC_REVIEW.md`, `docs/source/governance/AI_POLICY.md` Restricted AI Functions).

## Mission

To help human moderators and participants produce safe, structured, evidence-oriented dialogue and documentation, without ever substituting AI judgment for human moderation, expert perspective, or formal validation.

## Objectives

Support safe communication; improve documentation quality; improve signal clarity; reduce chronology and evidence gaps; support trauma-informed and ethical practice; support human moderators, never replace them.

## Scope

Applies to Structured Biography creation (Responsibility Biography Lab) and Structured Hearings, across all already-canonical Hearing Types (`STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`'s existing list: Individual, Community, Expert, Multi-Stakeholder, Institutional, Academic, Thematic, Policy, Digital, Hybrid) and delivery formats (in-person, online, hybrid). **No new hearing types are created here.** Digital, online, and hybrid are delivery formats, not separate evidence-validation processes. Expert, community, institutional, governance, and policy inputs remain contextual reflections or documentation perspectives (`STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`'s "Reflection ≠ Validation" clarification) — never scientific validation.

## Core Principles (Philosophy, continued)

1. **Never conducts the hearing** — the human Facilitator and Moderator remain in control at all times.
2. **Signal clarification, not emotional extraction** — every suggestion serves documentation purposes, never curiosity or depth-seeking.
3. **Advisory only** — consistent with the existing rule that AI assistance is "advisory only to the human moderator" (`AHIP.md`, `STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`).
4. **Safe hearing before complete hearing** — participant safety always takes precedence over documentation completeness.
5. **Human oversight always** — consistent with AI Governance Core Principle 2 (`AI_POLICY.md`).

## Definitions

**The AI Facilitator may:**
recommend the next safe question; identify missing topics relevant to documentation; identify chronology gaps; identify possible harm signals; identify missing institutional context; identify unclear responsibility pathways; identify missing evidence references; summarize what has been understood; suggest clarification questions; recommend breaks; monitor discussion balance; detect when one participant dominates; remind moderators of trauma-informed practice; remind moderators of ethical principles; recommend transitions between topics; identify unresolved issues for later documentation; prepare documentation summaries.

**The AI Facilitator must never:**
accuse; interrogate; pressure; manipulate; suggest answers; judge credibility; evaluate truthfulness; perform psychological diagnosis; force disclosure; optimize for data quantity over participant safety; turn the hearing into emotional exposure; replace human moderation.

**Hearing Facilitation Guide** — a dynamic artifact the AI Facilitator may generate, adapting to the hearing context. May include: suggested opening statements; participant briefing; moderator checklist; trauma-informed reminders; suggested follow-up questions; signal clarification prompts; timeline clarification prompts; institutional-context prompts; evidence-reference prompts; time management guidance; conflict de-escalation suggestions; documentation reminders; evidence collection reminders; closing checklist.

## Framework

**Signal-Oriented Guidance.** Every suggested question must serve a documented purpose: chronology clarification, evidence clarification, institutional clarification, responsibility clarification, harm mechanism clarification, governance impact clarification, missing information, contextual understanding, or repair relevance. **No suggested question may be asked merely to deepen emotional exposure.**

**Participant Safety.** The Guide must prioritize: dignity, voluntary participation, emotional safety, respect, inclusiveness, equal opportunity to speak, right to pause, right to skip, right to continue later. The objective is always: **safe hearing before complete hearing** — consistent with, and not superseding, `STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`'s existing safety protocol (participant may pause or end the session at any time; no session without a Moderator present).

**Adaptive Guidance.** The Guide adapts according to: biography completeness, participant needs, hearing objectives, hearing format (in-person/online/hybrid), available evidence, missing information, risk indicators, participant fatigue, cultural context, language, accessibility needs, documentation gaps, signal clarity.

**Relationship with Other Frameworks:** supports Responsibility Biography Lab (in a limited capacity — see Biography Lab reconciliation below) and Structured Hearings directly; its outputs feed Basic Validation Framework (completeness) and Hearing Quality Check (documentation/facilitation quality); it operates under `AI_POLICY.md`'s general AI Governance boundary. None of these is redefined here.

**Reconciliation with Responsibility Biography Lab — architecture correction.** `RESPONSIBILITY_BIOGRAPHY_LAB.md` previously stated "AI involvement... begins no earlier than AHIP." This capability extends AI assistance to the Biography Lab stage, but **narrowly**: only safety/comfort-oriented functions (recommending breaks, accessibility accommodations, trauma-informed reminders) apply there, consistent with that stage's existing principle that "nothing is filtered, categorized, or judged... intentionally minimal." The structuring-oriented functions (signal clarification, harm-mechanism identification, evidence-reference prompts, responsibility-pathway identification) do **not** apply at the Biography Lab stage — they begin at Structured Hearings, as before. `RESPONSIBILITY_BIOGRAPHY_LAB.md` is updated to reflect this narrow extension, not a wholesale reversal of its minimal-intervention principle.

## Workflow

No new lifecycle stage is introduced. The AI Facilitator operates *within* the existing Preparation and Moderation steps of Structured Hearings (`STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md` Workflow steps 1–2) and, narrowly, within Responsibility Biography Lab's existing single workflow step, generating and updating the Hearing Facilitation Guide as the session proceeds. It does not add, remove, or reorder any stage of the HARM Lifecycle, Structured Hearings' own Workflow, or Scientific Review's pipeline.

## Roles

The AI Facilitator supports, and is subordinate to: **Facilitator** (runs the session), **Moderator** (improves clarity/completeness, supports safety) — both roles as already defined in `STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`. It has no independent role or authority of its own.

## Inputs

The in-progress hearing or biography-creation session context; biography completeness state; available evidence; participant-stated needs and accessibility requirements.

## Outputs

Hearing Preparation Guide; Moderator Checklist; Suggested Question Flow; Signal Clarification Prompts; Documentation Checklist; Evidence Collection Checklist; Hearing Summary Draft; Follow-up Question Suggestions; Reflection Summary; Documentation Gap List. All outputs are advisory drafts for human review — none is published or acted upon without human sign-off, consistent with `STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`'s existing Governance ("no session content is published without subsequent verification and human sign-off").

## Governance

**Architecture — reusable platform capability, not a new lifecycle or hearing type.** It supports Structured Biography and evidence-oriented Structured Hearings. It does not replace human moderators. It does not replace experts. It does not replace community input. It does not replace Scientific Review. It does not replace Fact Check (referenced by name only — not yet a canonical stage in this repository, per the prior architecture consistency review; not invented or added here). It does not replace democratic deliberation. It always operates under human oversight.

## AI Integration

This document *is* the AI integration specification for hearing-based methodologies — self-referential, consistent with how `AI_POLICY.md` is self-referential for AI Governance generally. It operates within `AI_POLICY.md`'s existing Permitted AI Functions (draft recommendations, workflow automation, reporting assistance, evidence organization) and Restricted AI Functions (does not validate, does not decide) — no new AI function category is introduced.
