# AHIP

## Purpose
AHIP is the initial, human-moderated intake process that receives a citizen's account with trauma-informed care — the first structured step after Citizen Experience in the HARM Lifecycle.

## Background
Website-facing material (`.claude/skills/web-05-core-pages/SKILL.md`, `.claude/skills/web-01-sitemap/SKILL.md`) confirms AHIP is a real, named process with a dedicated public explanation page (`/methode`) and a corresponding RPCS specialization track ("AHIP Specialist"). No full AHIP specification document exists in the repository; this document reconstructs AHIP's role and boundaries from its confirmed position in the HARM Lifecycle and from the fragments available, and is marked accordingly.

**Status: Version 1.0 reconstruction.** The full intake protocol (exact intake questions, exact escalation criteria, exact session length) is not present in any available source and is not invented here — flagged as missing in the Migration Log.

## Core Principles
Trauma-informed care first; no judgment of the account at intake; safety before documentation.

## Definitions
AHIP receives a Citizen Experience account and prepares it for a Structured Hearing. It is intake, not evidence verification — no responsibility determination happens at this stage.

## Framework
Position in the HARM Lifecycle: `Citizen Experience → **AHIP** → Structured Hearing`.

## Workflow
1. Citizen shares an account through an intake channel.
2. A trained AHIP moderator receives it, applying trauma-informed practice.
3. The moderator assesses whether a Structured Hearing is appropriate and, if so, prepares the transition.

**Version 1.0 proposal — procedural detail, pending approval:** intake proceeds through structural categories rather than specific traumatic-content questions: (a) what happened, in the citizen's own words, with no interruption; (b) what support, if any, the citizen wants going forward; (c) a safety triage check (is there an immediate risk requiring escalation beyond the ordinary AHIP → Structured Hearing path). Any safety concern escalates immediately to a Moderator, per `../governance/ETHICS_CHARTER.md`'s Facilitator escalation rule, applied equally to AHIP moderators.

## Roles
**Moderator** (primary) — receives and assesses the account. **Participant** — shares the account.

## Inputs
A Citizen Experience account.

## Outputs
An account prepared for Structured Hearing, or a determination that a Structured Hearing is not appropriate at this time.

## Governance
Subject to Res Publica's trauma-informed language standard and data-protection requirements (see `../governance/DATA_POLICY.md`).

## AI Integration
AI does not perform intake moderation. Any AI assistance (e.g., language accessibility support) is advisory only to the human moderator.
