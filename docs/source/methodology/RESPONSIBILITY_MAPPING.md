# Responsibility Mapping Lab

## Purpose
Identifies who or what holds responsibility for the conditions or events a citizen's account describes — the second Innovation in the HARM framework. Canonical name: **Responsibility Mapping Lab** (UI label: "Mapping Lab").

## Background
Fully specified in `brain/FOUNDATION/01_HARM_OPERATING_SYSTEM.md` (Innovation 2) and reconciled here as its own canonical document.

## Core Principles
Evidence-grounded linkage only; no responsibility is asserted without a traceable basis.

## Definitions
A Responsibility Map is a structured linkage between an account and the entity or entities responsible for its conditions — not a new domain entity, a relationship view over Person and Organization records.

## Framework
- **Input:** one or more accounts from Listening/Citizen Experience.
- **Core Question:** "Who or what is responsible for this?"
- **Output:** a Responsibility Map.

## Workflow
1. Review the account for named or implied responsible parties.
2. Map to a Person or Organization record where identifiable.
3. Connect to existing Responsibility Evidence where a verifiable claim already exists.
4. Pass the map forward to the Responsibility Dashboard for aggregation.

## Roles
**Researcher** — conducts the mapping. **Reviewer** — checks the linkage before it is relied upon.

## Inputs
One or more Citizen Experience accounts.

## Outputs
A Responsibility Map.

## Governance
No individual is scored or ranked by how many maps reference them; mapping is descriptive, not evaluative of the mapped party's general standing.

## AI Integration
AI may assist in surfacing candidate Person/Organization matches for human confirmation; it does not finalize a mapping.
