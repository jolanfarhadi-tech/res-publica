# Structured Hearings

## Purpose
A Structured Hearing is the facilitated, prepared session where a citizen's account is explored in depth, under explicit safety and quality conditions, following AHIP intake.

## Background
Confirmed as a real, named process referenced across website material and the HARM Lifecycle. No full facilitation protocol document exists in the repository.

**Status: Version 1.0 reconstruction** for procedural detail (preparation checklist, exact safety protocol, exact evidence-capture format); the stage's existence, purpose, and position in the lifecycle are confirmed, not reconstructed.

## Core Principles
Safety before disclosure depth; preparation before the session; no re-traumatization.

## Definitions
Distinct from AHIP (intake) and Reflection (the pause immediately after). A Structured Hearing is the working session itself.

## Framework
Position in the HARM Lifecycle: `AHIP → **Structured Hearing** → Reflection`.

## Workflow
1. **Preparation** — the Facilitator reviews the AHIP-prepared account and plans the session.
2. **Moderation** — the session is run under trauma-informed facilitation practice.
3. **Evidence capture** — relevant details are documented as the session proceeds.
4. **Reflection handoff** — the account moves to the Reflection stage.

**Version 1.0 proposal — preparation and safety detail, pending approval:** preparation includes confirming the participant's availability and consent are current (not assumed from AHIP intake alone), reviewing any AHIP-flagged safety considerations, and confirming a Moderator is available for the session. Safety protocol proposes: the participant may pause or end the session at any time without justification; no session proceeds without a Moderator present; session length is set by participant comfort, not a fixed duration.

## Roles
**Facilitator** (primary) — runs the session. **Participant** — shares the account in depth. **Moderator** — supports safety.

## Inputs
An AHIP-prepared account.

## Outputs
A documented account, ready for Reflection.

## Governance
Subject to the organization's trauma-informed language standard; no session content is published without subsequent verification and human sign-off.

## AI Integration
AI does not facilitate or moderate hearings. AI is not present as a decision-maker in the session itself.
