# RPCS Program

## Purpose
RPCS ("Civic School") is Res Publica's structured learning and capacity-building program, preparing participants to take on operational roles within the HARM methodology.

## Background
Confirmed structure from website-facing material: 4 phases, 4 tracks, 5 levels, with a documented application process. No full program document exists in the repository.

**Status: Version 1.0 reconstruction** for phase/level content detail; program existence, structure counts, and application requirements are confirmed.

## Core Principles
Learning before competition (no gamified progression); capacity-building tied directly to real HARM roles, not abstract credentials.

## Definitions
- **Track** — a specialization within RPCS: Harm Explainer, AHIP Specialist, Codex Research, Trauma-Informed Facilitation.
- **Phase, Level** — structural progression units; see `RPCS_LEVELS.md`.

## Framework
4 phases × 4 tracks × 5 levels. *(Exact phase names/content and level criteria are not available in source material — Version 1.0 gap, not invented.)*

## Workflow
1. Application (see Inputs).
2. Track selection.
3. Progression through phases/levels.
4. Certification (see `RPCS_CERTIFICATION.md`).

## Roles
**Participant** (learner) → graduates into HARM operational roles (Facilitator, Researcher, Reviewer, etc.) matching their track.

## Inputs
Application fields, confirmed exactly: Name, Contact (email); Motivation (max. 300 words, client- and server-side validated); Track selection; Availability confirmation (1 online session/week + all 4 in-person Hearings).

## Outputs
Trained participants qualified for specific HARM operational roles.
