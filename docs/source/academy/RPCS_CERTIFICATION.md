# RPCS Certification

## Purpose
Describes what completing RPCS qualifies a participant to do within the HARM operating model.

## Background
No certification-specific document or fragment exists in the repository beyond the program's general existence.

**Status: Version 1.0 proposal.** No source material specifies certification criteria, format, or validity period. This document states only what can be reasonably inferred from RPCS's confirmed purpose (preparing participants for HARM operational roles) without inventing specific requirements.

## Core Principles
Certification reflects verified capability, never a purchased or automatically-granted credential; no certification is itself a public status symbol or ranking (Zero Gamification).

## Definitions
Certification is the confirmation that a participant has completed their chosen Track sufficiently to take on the corresponding HARM role (e.g., completing the "AHIP Specialist" track qualifies a participant for the Moderator role in AHIP intake).

## Framework
Track completion → role qualification.

**Version 1.0 proposal, pending approval:** a participant is certified upon (1) completing all levels of their chosen Track, (2) a Facilitator sign-off confirming demonstrated competence in a supervised session, and (3) no unresolved Ethics Charter concerns on record. This is a proposed process, not yet an approved certification standard.

## Workflow
1. Participant completes all levels of their Track (`RPCS_LEVELS.md`).
2. A Facilitator observes a supervised session and assesses demonstrated competence.
3. Ethics Charter compliance is confirmed (no unresolved concerns).
4. Certification is issued, qualifying the participant for the corresponding HARM operational role.

*(Proposed workflow, pending approval — not yet in effect.)*

## Roles
**Participant** receives certification. **Facilitator/Expert** (role unconfirmed) likely assesses completion.

## Inputs
Completed RPCS Track (all levels).

## Outputs
Qualification for a specific HARM operational role.

## Governance
No certification is issued without confirmed track/level completion; certification does not itself function as a reputation or ranking mechanism.
