# Philosophy

## Purpose
States the underlying philosophical commitments behind HARM and every Res Publica process — why the organization operates the way it does, not just what it does.

## Background
Synthesized from the organization's existing constitutional principles and the HARM Operating System's stated philosophy.

## Core Principles
Human dignity; accountability; knowledge before opinion; evidence before judgment; dialogue before ideology; learning before polarization; do no harm; transparency; collective intelligence; long-term societal resilience.

## Definitions
Each principle above governs a specific tension: e.g., "evidence before judgment" governs the tension between wanting to act quickly and needing to act correctly; "dialogue before ideology" governs the tension between taking a clear position and remaining genuinely open to being wrong.

## Framework
These principles are not independent — they compound. Human dignity without accountability produces sympathy without change; accountability without evidence produces blame without fairness; evidence without transparency produces expertise without trust.

## Workflow
Applied continuously, not as a checklist — every methodology document in this tree operationalizes one or more of these principles explicitly.

## Roles
Binds every role equally — no role is exempt from any principle.
