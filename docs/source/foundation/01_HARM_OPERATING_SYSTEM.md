# HARM Operating System

## Purpose

The HARM Operating System defines how Res Publica transforms lived civic experience into structured knowledge, validated responsibility evidence, institutional learning, and governance improvement. It is the organization's central operating methodology — not a piece of software, not a database, not an application. It describes the sequence every civic account moves through, from a citizen's first telling of an experience to the point where that experience has become part of the organization's collective, evidence-grounded civic intelligence.

## Background

Traditional civic participation formats — surveys, town halls, comment periods — collect opinions without verifying claims, without tracing responsibility to named accountable parties, and without a disciplined path from a single account to institutional learning. Res Publica's founding premise is that structured, evidence-based, trauma-informed civic listening — paired with rigorous verification and human-gated review — produces something traditional participation cannot: a durable, trustworthy record the organization and its partner institutions can actually act on. HARM exists to make that discipline repeatable rather than ad hoc.

## Core Principles

- **Human dignity** — every account is treated as a real person's lived experience, never raw data.
- **Accountability** — responsibility is traced to named, identifiable parties, never left diffuse.
- **Knowledge before opinion** — the system produces documented, structured knowledge, not aggregated sentiment.
- **Evidence before judgment** — no claim is treated as established until verified by someone other than its author.
- **Dialogue before ideology** — process design resists being captured by any single political framing.
- **Learning before polarization** — outputs are structured to produce shared understanding, not sides to take.
- **Do no harm** — the process itself (trauma-informed facilitation, careful language, no re-traumatization) is held to the same standard as its subject matter.
- **Transparency** — the methodology, its stages, and its evidentiary standards are documented and inspectable.
- **Collective intelligence** — the end goal is understanding the civic community builds together, not a conclusion imposed on it.
- **Long-term societal resilience** — HARM is designed to produce durable institutional learning, not a one-time report.

## Definitions

- **HARM** — the official name of Res Publica's operating methodology. It is a proper name, not an acronym to be expanded casually.
- **The HARM analytical engine (H-A-R-M)** — the four-part analytical lens applied within the methodology: **H**arm, **A**ccountability, **R**epair, **M**obilization (see Framework, below). This is a specific internal analytical tool, distinct from the overall methodology's name, though it shares the same four letters by design.
- **AHIP** — see `../methodology/AHIP.md`, not redefined here.
- **Structured Hearing** — see `../methodology/STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`, not redefined here.

## Framework

### The operating cycle

```
Citizen Experience
  ↓
AHIP
  ↓
Structured Hearing
  ↓
Reflection
  ↓
Expert Review
  ↓
Mechanism Identification
  ↓
Responsibility Evidence
  ↓
Knowledge Products
  ↓
Harm Codex
  ↓
Community Learning
  ↓
Institutional Learning
  ↓
Governance Improvement
```

- **Citizen Experience** — the raw, first-person account of something lived. Nothing is filtered or categorized yet.
- **AHIP** — the initial human-moderated intake process that receives the account with trauma-informed care. Detailed in `../methodology/AHIP.md`.
- **Structured Hearing** — a facilitated, prepared session where the account is explored in depth under safety and quality conditions. Detailed in `../methodology/STRUCTURED_HEARINGS.md`.
- **Reflection** — a deliberate pause for the participant and facilitators to consider the account before it moves to external review, protecting against premature conclusions.
- **Expert Review** — domain experts assess the account's substance, context, and any technical claims it contains.
- **Mechanism Identification** — the underlying systemic or institutional mechanism producing the harm is identified, distinct from any single incident.
- **Responsibility Evidence** — the account, once substantiated, becomes a verified evidence record naming who or what holds responsibility.
- **Knowledge Products** — Responsibility Evidence and its supporting material are turned into structured outputs (see `../methodology/RESPONSIBILITY_DASHBOARD.md`, `../methodology/RESPONSIBILITY_ANNEXES.md`).
- **Harm Codex** — recurring mechanisms and patterns are entered into the organization's standing taxonomy of documented harms. Detailed in `../methodology/HARM_CODEX.md`.
- **Community Learning** — the community closest to the account gains shared understanding of the pattern.
- **Institutional Learning** — the institutions named in Responsibility Evidence are presented with the documented pattern.
- **Governance Improvement** — the cycle's intended end-state: a concrete change in institutional practice or policy.

**Resolved:** the 12-stage cycle above is the canonical HARM Lifecycle. The website's "8 steps" reference is a simplified public presentation of the same cycle, not a distinct or competing version — no further confirmation is needed on this point.

### The HARM analytical engine

| Letter | Concept | Function |
|---|---|---|
| H | Harm | Names the actual harm described — what occurred, to whom, how severe. |
| A | Accountability | Establishes who holds responsibility and whether it is acknowledged. |
| R | Repair | Identifies what would remedy the harm, and whether repair has been attempted. |
| M | Mobilization | Identifies what collective civic action could support repair. |

## Workflow

1. A citizen shares an account (Citizen Experience).
2. AHIP intake receives it with trauma-informed care.
3. A Structured Hearing explores it in depth.
4. A Reflection period follows before external review.
5. Expert Review assesses substance and context.
6. The underlying Mechanism is identified.
7. The account becomes Responsibility Evidence once verified by a named reviewer distinct from its source.
8. Knowledge Products (Dashboard entries, Annexes) are produced.
9. Recurring mechanisms enter the Harm Codex.
10. Community and Institutional Learning follow.
11. Governance Improvement is the intended outcome.

Each step requires the previous step's output — no step is skipped, and no step reorders another.

## Roles

- **Participant** — shares an account; participates in Reflection.
- **Witness** — corroborates an account.
- **Moderator** — ensures AHIP and Structured Hearing sessions meet safety and quality standards.
- **Researcher** — conducts Mechanism Identification and Harm Codex research.
- **Reviewer** — verifies Responsibility Evidence; must not be the account's own source.
- **Facilitator** — runs Structured Hearings with trauma-informed practice.
- **Community Leader** — supports Community Learning within their community.
- **Expert** — conducts Expert Review.
- **Observer** — monitors process integrity without authoring content.
- **Institution** — the accountable party named in Responsibility Evidence; participates in Institutional Learning and Governance Improvement.

Responsibilities only — permission levels are not defined here; see `../governance/AI_POLICY.md` and Res Publica's approved Permission Matrix.

## Inputs

A citizen's account, shared through any Res Publica touchpoint; corroborating witness accounts; expert domain knowledge; existing Harm Codex entries (for pattern-matching during Mechanism Identification).

## Outputs

Responsibility Evidence records; Knowledge Products (Dashboard entries, Annexes); Harm Codex entries; Community and Institutional Learning materials; Governance Improvement recommendations.

## Governance

HARM operates entirely within Res Publica's existing constitutional principles — zero gamification (no part of this cycle scores, ranks, or compares people or accounts), accountability traced to named humans, and human sign-off required at every verification step. See `../foundation/04_GOVERNANCE.md` and `../governance/ETHICS_CHARTER.md`. HARM does not itself define governance — it operates under it.

## AI Integration

AI assists at defined points (e.g., surfacing related Harm Codex entries during Mechanism Identification, drafting summary language for Reflection) but never performs Expert Review, never verifies Responsibility Evidence, never determines responsibility, and never makes a Governance Improvement decision. Human approval is mandatory at every verification and decision point. See `../foundation/05_AI.md`.
