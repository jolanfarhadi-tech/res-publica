# Ethics Charter

## Purpose
States the ethical commitments specific to HARM's work with lived, often traumatic civic experience — beyond the general organizational principles in `../foundation/03_VALUES.md`.

## Background
Referenced as an expected document (`docs/source/core/03_ETHICS_CHARTER.md`) in website-facing project material, but no content for it exists anywhere in the repository.

**Status: Version 1.0 proposal**, built only from already-approved, existing principles (trauma-informed language, no exploitation of testimony, no commercialization of testimony) — no new ethical framework is introduced.

## Core Principles
No exploitation of participants; no commercialization of testimony; trauma-informed language always; no re-traumatization; no political manipulation of accounts.

## Definitions
**Exploitation**, in this context, means using a participant's account for organizational benefit (funding, publicity, political positioning) without their informed, ongoing consent and without benefit flowing back to addressing their harm.

## Framework
Applies at every HARM Lifecycle stage, especially AHIP, Structured Hearings, and any public-facing Knowledge Product.

**Version 1.0 proposal — specific rules, pending approval:**
1. **Consent is renewed, not assumed.** A participant's consent to share an account does not automatically extend to its use in a Knowledge Product, Harm Codex entry, or public Civic Intelligence Report — each new use requires a fresh, explicit consent check.
2. **Right to withdraw at any stage.** A participant may withdraw their account from further processing at any point before publication, without needing to justify the decision.
3. **Facilitator escalation path.** A Facilitator who identifies a safety concern during a Structured Hearing escalates to a Moderator immediately, pausing the session rather than continuing under the assumption the concern will resolve itself.
4. **Reviewer conflict of interest.** A Reviewer with any personal or institutional relationship to the account's subject matter must recuse themselves; this extends the existing Responsibility Evidence rule (Reviewer ≠ Contributor) to conflicts beyond direct authorship.

## Workflow
Every Knowledge Product is checked against this Charter before publication, alongside its ordinary human sign-off.

## Roles
**Reviewer, Moderator, Facilitator** — hold primary responsibility for upholding this Charter at their respective stages.

## Inputs
Any account, Knowledge Product, or public communication involving participant testimony.

## Outputs
A published item confirmed consistent with this Charter, or a rejected/revised one.

## Governance
This Charter may not be weakened by any other document; it is amendable only through the organization's formal governance process (`../foundation/04_GOVERNANCE.md`).

## AI Integration
AI-assisted drafting is subject to the same Charter — an AI-drafted summary of testimony must meet the same non-exploitation and trauma-informed standard as human-drafted material.
